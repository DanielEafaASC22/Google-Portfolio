Dear Grandpa

Shakesperian Sonnet


Time withered away painful memories,
But some memories you never forget.
Now I am just left with your lemures.
Now the family is just a quartet.

All the soccer games we watched together,
And all of the walks that we have taken.
Those days we would spend time on whatever.
Also the time you almost ate bacon.

Now I can only think about the times
when you were right here: always by my side.
Need change for a buck? You’d hand me 10 dimes.
You were always there whenever I cried.

Now I must say goodbye, even if it stings.
Your soul shall blossom like lilies in spring.
